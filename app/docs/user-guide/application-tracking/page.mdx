# Application tracking

When a job moves into the **Applied** stage, Ascendio unlocks a dedicated panel for tracking everything about your application — from how you applied to when you expect to hear back. The goal is to have everything in one place so you never lose context.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/application-tracking/overview.png"
  alt="Application tracking panel"
  caption="The Applied stage panel — all your application details in one place"
/>

## How it works

The Applied panel is organized into four sections: the application details chips at the top, follow-ups, expected response, and notes. Each section is independent and can be filled in at any time.

<Callout type="tip">
  Click any chip to edit it. Press **Save** in the top right when you are done
  making changes.
</Callout>

## Follow-ups

The follow-ups section helps you stay on top of responses after applying. You can schedule follow-up dates using the quick shortcuts or a custom date picker.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/application-tracking/follow-ups.png"
  alt="Follow-ups section"
  caption="Schedule follow-ups with +3 days, +7 days, or a custom date"
/>

There are three ways to add a follow-up date:

- **+3 days** — schedules a follow-up 3 days from today
- **+7 days** — schedules a follow-up 7 days from today
- **Custom** — opens a date picker so you can choose any date

The section shows your **last follow-up date** and your **next scheduled follow-up**. When a follow-up date has passed without a response, the status changes from **On track** to **Overdue**.

<Callout type="warning">
  If a follow-up is overdue, it will be highlighted on the job card on the
  Kanban board so you can spot it immediately without opening the job.
</Callout>

## Expected response

Set a date by which you expect to hear back from the employer. This helps you know when it is reasonable to follow up or move on.

Click **Not set — click to add** to open the date picker and choose an expected response date.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/application-tracking/expected-response.png"
  alt="Expected response date picker"
  caption="Set an expected response date to know when to follow up"
/>

## Notes

The notes field is a free-text area for anything relevant to this application — things you want to remember, questions to ask, or context about the role.

Click **Click to add notes...** to start typing. Notes are saved when you press **Save**.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/application-tracking/notes.png"
  alt="Notes section"
  caption="Use notes to capture anything relevant to this application"
/>

## Wishlist research

Below the Applied panel, Ascendio preserves the research notes you wrote when the job was in the **Wishlist** stage. This gives you full context — the pros, cons, company culture notes, and skills gap analysis you recorded before applying — without having to go back and find them.

<Callout type="info">
  Wishlist research is read-only once a job moves to Applied. It is there for
  reference, not editing.
</Callout>
