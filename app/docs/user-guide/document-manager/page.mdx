# Document manager

The Document manager is where you upload, organize, and manage your CVs and cover letters. Every document you upload becomes available to attach to job applications — and your default documents are pre-selected automatically when you apply.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/document-manager/overview.png"
  alt="Document manager page"
  caption="The Document manager — upload on top, your documents below, stats on the right"
/>

## Uploading a document

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/document-manager/upload.png"
  alt="Upload documents form"
  caption="Upload a CV or cover letter as a PDF — max 5MB"
/>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the document type">
    Choose whether you are uploading a **CV** or a **Cover letter**. This determines how the document is categorized and which applications it can be attached to.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your file">
    Click **Choose File** and select a PDF from your device. Only PDF files are supported, with a maximum size of 5MB per file.

    <Callout type="tip">
      Name your file clearly before uploading — for example `Software_Engineer_CV.pdf` or `Cover_Letter_Anthropic.pdf`. The filename is displayed throughout the app and makes it easier to identify which version you sent where.
    </Callout>

  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload">
    Click **Upload** to save the document. It will appear in the Your Documents section below.

    <Callout type="info">
      If the document does not appear immediately after uploading, click the **Refresh** button in the Your Documents section to reload the list.
    </Callout>

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Document stats

On the right side of the page, a stats panel gives you an overview of your document library across three tabs.

### Defaults

Shows your currently set default CV and cover letter — the documents that are pre-selected when a job moves to Applied. Each card shows the filename, file size, and an **Advanced** section with additional details.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/document-manager/defaults.png"
  alt="Default documents tab"
  caption="Your default CV and cover letter — pre-selected for every new application"
/>

<Callout type="tip">
  Always keep your defaults up to date. If you update your CV, upload the new
  version and set it as the default so it is automatically used for new
  applications going forward.
</Callout>

### Chart

A pie chart showing the breakdown of your uploaded documents by type — how many CVs versus cover letters you have uploaded in total.

A storage usage breakdown showing your total storage used, average file size, and how much of your 20MB storage limit you have consumed.

  uploads and previews.
</Callout>

## Default documents and applications

When a job moves from **Wishlist** to **Applied**, Ascendio automatically pre-selects your default CV and cover letter in the application details. You can change these at any time from within the job — the defaults are a starting point, not a constraint.

<Callout type="tip">
  If you apply with different CV versions for different roles — for example a
  front-end focused CV versus a full-stack CV — upload both and switch between
  them per application rather than changing your default each time.
</Callout>
