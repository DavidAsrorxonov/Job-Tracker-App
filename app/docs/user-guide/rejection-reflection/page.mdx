# Rejection reflection

When a job moves into the **Rejected** stage, Ascendio gives you a structured space to process what happened and extract something useful from it. A rejection is data — the more honestly you record it, the more it helps you improve.

## Rejection details

The first section captures the facts of the rejection — when it happened, at what stage, why, and any feedback received.

### Rejection date

The date you received the rejection. Use the date picker to set it.

### Rejection reason

A free-text field for the reason given by the employer. If they said they went with a more experienced candidate, a better culture fit, or gave no reason at all — record it here.

### Feedback

Any constructive feedback received from the recruiter or interviewer. Even a brief comment is worth capturing — it can reveal patterns across multiple rejections.

<Callout type="tip">
  Not all employers give feedback. If you received none, leave this blank. If
  you did, even a single sentence is worth recording.
</Callout>

## Reflection

The second section is about you — turning the rejection into something useful for your next application.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/rejection-reflection/reflection.png"
  alt="Reflection section"
  caption="Turn the rejection into a learning opportunity"
/>

### Lessons learned

A free-text field for what you would do differently next time. Be specific — vague notes like "prepare more" are less useful than "should have practiced system design questions and prepared more concrete examples of past projects."

<Callout type="info">
  Over time, your lessons learned across multiple rejections will reveal
  patterns — whether it is interview preparation, CV presentation, or role
  targeting. The data is only useful if you record it honestly.
</Callout>

### Would you reapply?

A simple yes or no — would you consider applying to this company again in the future? This helps you quickly identify companies worth revisiting when you are ready to apply again.

### Reapply date

If you answered yes to reapplying, set a date for when you would consider doing so. This is a personal reminder — only visible to you and only relevant if you selected yes.

<Callout type="tip">
  Use the reapply date to set a realistic timeframe — for example six months or
  a year from now. Companies often rehire for the same role after a candidate
  pool changes.
</Callout>

## Previous stage notes

Below the reflection section, all previous stage data remains accessible — interview rounds, application details, and wishlist research. These provide full context for understanding why the rejection happened.

<Callout type="info">
  Nothing is deleted when a job moves to Rejected. Every note, interview round,
  and detail from earlier stages is preserved and accessible at the bottom of
  the page.
</Callout>
