# Timeline

The Timeline gives you a complete, chronological view of everything that has happened to a job — from the moment you added it to the latest update.

Every stage change, interview, note, and follow-up is recorded as an event. This allows you to understand not just the current state of a job, but the entire journey that led there.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/timeline/overview.png"
  alt="Timeline overview"
  caption="The Timeline page — a full history of activity for a job"
/>

## Activity overview

At the top of the page, you will see a summary of your activity for this job.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/timeline/activity-overview.png"
  alt="Activity overview cards"
  caption="Quick metrics showing total activity and key actions"
/>

These cards give you a quick snapshot:

- **Total events** — the number of recorded actions
- **Stage changes** — how many times the job moved between stages
- **Interviews** — total interview-related events
- **Follow-ups** — reminders or follow-up actions you created

<Callout type="info">
  This section helps you quickly understand how active or complex a job process
  has been without scrolling through the full timeline.
</Callout>

---

## Activity insights

Below the summary, you will find visual analytics of your activity.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/timeline/activity-insights.png"
  alt="Charts and analytics"
  caption="Charts showing how your activity evolves over time"
/>

### Activity over time

A bar chart showing how many events happened on each day. This helps you identify when you were most active with this job.

### Event breakdown

A distribution of activity types — such as stage changes, interviews, and follow-ups.

### Activity intensity

A visual comparison of how different activity types contribute to your workflow.

### Cumulative activity

A running total of all events over time. This shows how your engagement with the job has progressed.

<Callout type="tip">
  Use these insights to understand your behavior — for example, whether you are
  following up consistently or preparing adequately before interviews.
</Callout>

---

## Timeline feed

The main section of the page is the timeline itself — a chronological feed of all events.

<ImageCaption
  src="/docs/timeline/timeline-feed.png"
  alt="Timeline feed"
  caption="Events displayed in chronological order"
/>

Each entry represents a specific action or update.

### Structure of an event

Each event includes:

- **Type badge** (e.g. Interview, Stage Change)
- **Title** — what happened
- **Description** — additional context
- **Timestamp** — exact time and relative time (e.g. “4 days ago”)

Events are grouped by date to make the timeline easier to scan.

---

## Event types

Different types of actions are recorded in the timeline:

- **Stage changes** — when the job moves between stages
- **Interviews** — scheduled, completed, or updated interviews
- **Follow-ups** — reminders or actions you created
- **Notes / updates** — additional changes or metadata updates

<Callout type="info">
  The timeline is automatically generated — you don’t need to manually log
  events. Every meaningful action in the job lifecycle is recorded.
</Callout>

---

## Why the timeline matters

The Timeline is more than just history — it helps you:

- See how long each stage took
- Identify gaps (e.g. no follow-ups after applying)
- Understand patterns across your applications
- Reflect on your process and improve it

<Callout type="tip">
  Reviewing your timeline after a job ends — especially after rejection — can
  reveal what to improve next time.
</Callout>
